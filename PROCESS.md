# DREC Evaluation Process

This summarises the Standard Operating Protocol. The authoritative documents are the DREC Guidelines (DU-DoC-DREC-2025-01) and the Evaluation Protocol.

## 1. Triage - which pathway?

The Member Secretary classifies each application before review:

1. No primary data AND no identifiable personal data? -> Self-declaration (Form DREC-02).
2. Sensitive data (Cat B), identifiable secondary data (Cat C), cross-border (Cat D), dual-use (Cat E), or above minimal risk? -> Full board review (External Member required).
3. Minimal-risk and exemption-eligible, or low-risk human-participant study? -> Expedited review.
4. Otherwise -> refer to the Chairperson; escalate when in doubt.

Note on Category C: a licence-restricted dataset with no personal identifiers (e.g. PROWESS, Bloomberg under institutional licence) is handled by expedited review. Full board applies where identifiable personal information is present. This routing is a deliberate departmental interpretation, ratified by the DREC.

## 2. Completeness check

An application is reviewed only when complete. Incomplete applications are returned without review.

## 3. Scoring

Reviewers score against nine criteria: scientific merit; voluntary participation; informed consent; risk-benefit balance; privacy and confidentiality; data security; vulnerable populations; AI and digital ethics; legal compliance.

## 4. Decision

- All criteria met -> Approved (Ethical Clearance Certificate issued; valid 24 months)
- Only minor revisions -> Approved with conditions (comply within 15 working days)
- Any major concern -> Revise and resubmit (re-reviewed within 3 weeks)
- Irremediable concern -> Rejected (right of appeal within 21 days)

Decisions are communicated in writing within five working days of the review meeting.

## 5. Transparency

Every decision is recorded, anonymised, in DECISIONS_REGISTER.md. The full signed records are retained confidentially by the Member Secretary for at least five years.
