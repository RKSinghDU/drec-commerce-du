"# DREC Evaluation Process\n\nThis summarises the Standard Operating Protocol. The authoritative documents are the DREC Guidelines (DU-DoC-DREC-2025-01) and the Evaluation Protocol.\n\n## 1. Triage - which pathway?\n\nThe Member Secretary classifies each application before review:\n\n1. No primary data AND no identifiable personal data? -> Self-declaration (Form DREC-02).\n2. Sensitive data (Cat B), identifiable secondary data (Cat C), cross-border (Cat D), dual-use (Cat E), or above minimal risk? -> Full board review (External Member required).\n3. Minimal-risk and exemption-eligible, or low-risk human-participant study? -> Expedited review.\n4. Otherwise -> refer to the Chairperson; escalate when in doubt.\n\nNote on Category C: a licence-restricted dataset with no personal identifiers (e.g. PROWESS, Bloomberg under institutional licence) is handled by expedited review. Full board applies where identifiable personal information is present. This routing is a deliberate departmental interpretation, ratified by the DREC.\n\n## 2. Completeness check\n\nAn application is reviewed only when complete. Incomplete applications are returned without review.\n\n## 3. Scoring\n\nReviewers score against nine criteria: scientific merit; voluntary participation; informed consent; risk-benefit balance; privacy and confidentiality; data security; vulnerable populations; AI and digital ethics; legal compliance.\n\n## 4. Decision\n\n- All criteria met -> Approved (Ethical Clearance Certificate issued; valid 24 months)\n- Only minor revisions -> Approved with conditions (comply within 15 working days)\n- Any major concern -> Revise and resubmit (re-reviewed within 3 weeks)\n- Irremediable concern -> Rejected (right of appeal within 21 days)\n\nDecisions are communicated in writing within five working days of the review meeting.\n\n## 5. Transparency\n\nEvery decision is recorded, anonymised, in DECISIONS_REGISTER.md. The full signed records are retained confidentially by the Member Secretary for at least five years.\n"
